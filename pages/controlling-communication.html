Controlling Communication

By: Shawn Kiruba

Communication in a project management team

 Communication between members of a project management team can be very challenging but also bring about the best in everyone, which will provide the best product for whichever project the team is working towards. There are many things to keep in mind when it comes to communication, you always need to make sure that the team is in constant communication with not only each other but also the stakeholders, customers, the higher management to make sure that they are creating a deliverable that is not only efficient but also desired by the customers and stakeholders. Team members have to be effective and can also only be effective if the team makes sure that they are not taking time away from their tasks, which is the main goal of communication within a project team.

Poor communication within the team can cause project failure

 Some of the things that will get affected when a team has poor communication are that there would be a lot of misunderstandings about the goals and objectives of the project. The team will also be prone to missing a lot of deadlines because those dates were possibly never communicated with the team. With the lack of communication, there can also be conflicts between team members, certain members in the team heading in different directions of the project, decreasing the productivity of each team member which in return will mean that the project budget might have to be raised, and finally, there will be a lack of commitment to the project which will certainly decrease the productivity and may even cause project failure.

Poor communication with stakeholders can cause project failure

 Some of the things that will get affected when a team has poor communication with their stakeholders are that there is a lack of or there is limited buy-in for the project, this would mean that there might be a chance that the project will not have enough funding or resources. One of the main things that lead to the success of a project is you can meet the expectations of the stakeholders, not being able to communicate with them would surely lead to failure. Another major thing is that without communication with the stakeholders, they will not be involved with the project which will mean that you have less help with it comes to getting the project accomplished and that will eventually lead to the failure of the project.

Communication planning

 Some things that can help with communication planning between the project team, stakeholders, and other involved parties are very simple. The first question that you should ask your team is who will you be communicating with throughout the project, this can be done by making a list of all the people that are involved in the project and all the people that the team needs to be in constant communication with. The second thing that the team should understand is the information that will be communicated, this can also be written down and when you do get to the meeting, you can communicate with the parties accordingly. The third thing that you will need to think about is when and how you will communicate. During this time, there are times when certain members or parties are not able to make it to meetings, if it is possible the team can make the meeting online and at a time that is suitable for everyone. Simply being able to communicate with each other will help you understand and get down most of these steps down, if you meet with the members of the team make sure to talk about the next time the whole team, stakeholders, and other parties are meeting and where they are all meeting, this will make the planning process much easier.

Ways of communicating with multiple parties

 In the day and age that we live in, there are many ways to communicate between many different parties. The first way of communication can just be a one-on-one meeting where a project manager can meet with a stakeholder to discuss the future direction of the project or if certain things need to be changed or updated in the project so far. Another way of communicating would be to meet in small groups to discuss the project. These meetings are very important but they are also a very formal kind of meeting, the reason for these meetings is to communicate information about the project, so it does not matter how these parties meet. For example, team members and other stakeholders can even meet during lunch times to discuss the project. If the reason for the meeting is meant to be short or if there is just some sort of a question between team members or certain stakeholders, the best case of communication would be things like email, or virtual meetings that are planned beforehand.

Challenges that come with long-distance members

 Some of the challenges that come with having stakeholders or team members abroad would just be able to find the time to communicate because of the time difference or being able to meet because of the distance. This can be easily solved with software like Zoom, Skype, and Webex that help make these kinds of meetings possible. The team members and stakeholders can talk about a time when they are all free regarding the time difference and once they find the time when they are all able to meet, they can all create a virtual meeting that will help them communicate face-to-face about all the discussion topics to ensure the success of the project.

Conclusion

 In conclusion, making sure that you are able to communicate with all the members that are involved in the project is very important. With a lack of communication, the members of the project can head off track which will then eventually lead to each member heading in a different direction for the project and the stakeholders also not being able to get what they want. With communication, whether it is virtual or in-person, communicating between the members and stakeholders will establish a ground where everyone involved in the project will know where the project is heading and they will also know where they are supposed to head towards.
