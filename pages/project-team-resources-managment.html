Project Team Management: tools for better managing team resources

One of the most crucial aspects of working in an Agile environment in any project is teamwork. Good teamwork and cooperation is imperative in fields such as software development, because most projects in these fields tend to be large-scale and complex. Just imagine the number of people working on projects such as videogames, search engines, or AI software, these teams may have a strength of over a hundred people with millions of dollars in investment. When the stakes are that high, all precautions must be undertaken to ensure the project goes smoothly. Therefore, to ensure the success of a project, good teamwork and skills and technologies that improve teamwork are essential.

Some people may be reluctant to work in teams. This may be due to their negative past experiences with poor or ineffective teamwork. Nonetheless, at some point they will have to participate in teams, since teamwork is so important. To ensure that future team efforts are successful, it is important to understand how to guarantee effective teamwork. All team members must have a clear understanding of the project and have clear communication between themselves. A team leader should promote this understanding and facilitate good communication, so that every team member is familiar with the direction of the project.

This blog post endeavors to explore some tools, techniques and strategies for project team and resource management with the hope that a better understanding of those will result in increased productivity and innovation.

Choosing A Leader

A team leader plays a vital role in the success of a team. This is because they act as a central point for communications and task management. Not only do they assign and delegate tasks, but they also provide guidance. Since it is such an important role, a leader must be someone who has earned their team’s trust. They should also have excellent soft skills and leaderships skills. Sometimes, the leadership role may be rotated among team members, in order to ensure each member gets a chance at leadership development. That may allow for more collaboration and mutual understanding. A good leader also observes the strengths of their team members and utilizes those while understanding their weaknesses and encouraging improvement there. Good leaders are essential to the success of projects.

Communication

Communication is the most important aspect of teamwork if not one of the most important. It is crucial to the success of projects because poor communication may lead to lower productivity, higher cost, and projects being delivered overdue. Furthermore, communication between technical and non-technical colleagues within the project may be strained by the use of jargon. It is important to understand that communication skills play just as important a role as technical skills in the success of a project.

Good communication is promoted by focusing on individual and group needs, using the correct medium for communication at the right place and time, and distributing information in an effective and timely manner. Adding too many people in a team to try and speed up a task may be counterproductive as more people create more communication channels and more chance for miscommunication. Moreover, if an email is sent out when a real time meeting was required or a meeting was held for something that could have been an email, it may result in loss of time and money. Lastly, information should reach its destination at the right time and without being altered, otherwise, this may also cause losses. A lot more can be said about communication, because it is essential to the success of any project.

Understanding what motivates employees

What motivates us to do what we do? Motivational theories attempt to answer this very question. Motivation may be divided in two kinds, intrinsic and extrinsic. Intrinsic motivation is that which comes from within and motivates people for their own enjoyment. Extrinsic motivation comes from external rewards or punishments. It's important to understand motivational theory to work effectively with others.

Gantt Charts

Projects that span a long period of time may utilize Gantt Charts to create a clear and detailed work schedule. Often, these charts are used in proposals to demonstrate to stakeholders that the team has a well-organized and viable plan for delivering the project. Gantt Charts can also be used in progress reports to inform the reader which tasks have been completed, which are in progress, and which are still not done.

Gantt Charts come in many different forms, and complex and detailed charts can be created using software (examples are available on the Wikipedia page for Gantt Charts). Team Charters

A team charter is a way for teams to describe and define the different details of a project, such as purpose, duration and scope. The idea is to help team members to all be on the same page and concur with each other. It helps misunderstandings from arising in the future by defining expectation early on. Its points can be negotiated so that everyone agrees on what is being added to the charter. It also lists the tools and strategies that will be utilized in the scenario of something going wrong.

Training and Team-building Activities

To build effective teams, organizations offer in-house training or hire external companies specialized in team-building activities. It's crucial to consider individual needs, such as learning styles and physical limitations, when selecting team-building options. These activities may be physical or mental. These team-building activities aim to help team members to understand each other's differences and work together effectively.
