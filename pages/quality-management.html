Quality Management

By: Shawn Kiruba

What is Quality Management?

 Quality management is when an organization wants to ensure that all the products and services they offer are of their best quality. They ensure that they can keep the level of quality that they prefer for the company and their products to their customers. The organization and the agile teams do what they can within their power to make sure that they keep a steady level of quality within their products and services. There are many key components when it comes to quality management.

Key quality one: Quality Planning

 The first key quality of quality management is quality planning. Quality planning means that the organization meets to understand and identify the standards of quality that are needed for the project and decide the best approach to achieving that outcome.

Key quality two: Quality Improvement

 The second key quality of quality management is quality improvement. Quality improvement is when the organization meets to find a change that benefits the project and the quality of the project. They not only make sure that the quality of the project is better but also the reliability or success of that demanded quality to succeed.

Key quality three: Quality Control

 The third key quality of quality management is quality control. Quality control is when the organization meets to make sure that all the employees and workers that are working on the project are contributing to what they need to do and that they are upholding the integrity of the process to achieve the outcome that they desire.

Key quality four: Quality Assurance

 The fourth and final quality of quality management is quality assurance. Quality assurance is when the organization sets a number of standards and planned things to do to make sure that the reliability of the product or service they are trying to deliver will meet the requirements that the project manager set out to go with at the start.

Principles of Quality Management

 There are many principles that are considered the standard for quality management. These principles are used by many project managers around the world to ensure their organization's success. These principles are also used by top management so that they can make sure that the organization's performance increases throughout the years.

Principle one: Customer Focus

 Customer focus is something that is important to any business or organization. This primary focus makes sure that a company can release products that are catered to their customers and this will benefit the company as a whole because when they release products that are catered to their customers, that will in return increase customer satisfaction and loyalty. This loyalty will then make sure that they invest in the company in the long run which will then give the company money in revenue. This business that makes sure customer focus is important to them also has better quality products and processes that are more efficient than their competitors.

Principle two: Leadership

 A good leader in any group is a vital part of the team's success. In an organization, good leadership can make sure that the employees are staying motivated in what they are doing as well as many sure that the workforce works with a purpose. With a purpose, they will work more efficiently and also produce products that are of higher quality.

Principle three: Engagement of People

 Involvement of the whole organization and getting input from others and hearing other parties out is a principle that is very important to the success of a project. Making sure that all of your workforce, including the ones that are part-time and full-time, are working efficiently and are provided with everything they need will make sure that they succeed in what they are given to do. A project team would always encourage each other to make sure that they are giving their best in what they do as well as making sure that if they need help with anything that is personal they are able to get that help in the form of friendships or financial support.

Principle four: Process Approach

 Something that is crucial to the success of a project is the way that a project team approaches the project that they were given. The way that they approach can determine how efficiently they will be able to complete that project. Making sure that the approach goes over all the details and the workforce is given the resources that they need will make sure that they can decrease future cost spending as well as waste time.

Principle five: Continuous Improvement

 Any organization that is working on a project should have a clear objective that they need to be working towards continuous improvement. If they are working in agile ways and doing sprints, they need to make sure that they are also doing retrospectives to make sure that they can improve from each sprint, and to do that they need to, from the start, have a clear continuous improvement objective.

Principle six: Evidence-based Decision Making

 Decision-making when it is based on assumptions is never going to always give you a positive response. When an organization starts making decisions that are based on evidence that has happened previously, then they will be able to analyze those mistakes and errors made from previous projects and they will ensure that they donâ€™t make those same mistakes again. With this, it will decrease the number of times unintended results appears.

Principle seven: Relationship Management

 Relationship management is something that a company can do as a whole where they are able to make relationships with not only the customers and stakeholders but also the suppliers and retailers that sell their products and services. When an organization begins to form relationships between its suppliers and retailers it will be able to optimize their impact and promote its products better as well. With that in mind when after forming this relationships the company will be able to increase its performance and also be able to manage the parties that are involved with its product which will also ensure that the customers are getting what they wanted and that will increase customer satisfaction and loyalty.
